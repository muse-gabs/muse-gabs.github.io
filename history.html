History

BMW stands for Bayerische Motoren Werke AG which is equivalent to Bavarian Motor Works when translated from German to English. Founded in 1916, it originally produced aircraft engines. The first engine being a straight-six aircraft engine. After World War I, BMW produced motorcycle engines, farm equipment, household items and railway brakes. The picture above is the BMW motorcycle that was utilized in the British racing scene by drivers Walter Schneider and Hans Strauss in 1958.
When thinking about the BMW car, you often hear the terms "beamer," "beemer," and "bimmer", when discussing the automobile, the correct term is "bimmer" or "beemer", "beamer" solely refers to the BMW motorcycle. "Beamer" comes from Great Britain and was used to distinguish it from the British manufacturer "Beezer". The nickname "bimmer" originated in the 1970s, when BMW automobiles became popular in the US. It wasn't until the Boston Chapter magazine for BMW fans was published did "bimmer" win out the other nicknames and has now become the term for BMW automobiles all over the world.
